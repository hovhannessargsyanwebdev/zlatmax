A light, convenient knife with a blade having gentle concave lenticular descents two-thirds of the width of the blade, forming a thin, perfectly cutting blade with a thickness of about 0.6 mm in the area of the drains.


The butt of the blade with the back of the handle has one smooth arc-shaped line. On the heel in front of the handle there is a sub-finger cutout for precise work.


It is necessary to exercise some caution when working with large lateral loads on the blade of the knife, due to the small thickness of the blade in the working part.


Mounting of the handle of the patch patch. On the top of the handle, the function of which is performed by the shank protruding from under the dies, there is a hole for the strap. This knife is a convenient assistant when working with food and hunting for hog and waterfowl.
